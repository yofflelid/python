Among those, drought and high salinity are frequent and these greatly affect on plant growth, development and productivity. Plant hormone abscisic acid (ABA) has key roles in regulating plant responses to abiotic stresses as well as in controlling seed development, dormancy, germination and vegetative growth. ABA levels are increased dramatically in response to drought and high salinity, resulting in the induction of defense genes conferring stress tolerance. The increase of endogenous ABA level is mainly determined by the rate of ABA biosynthesis. Nine-cis-epoxycarotenoid dioxygenase (NCED) is known as the rate-limiting enzyme in ABA biosynthesis and has an important role in responses to abiotic stresses.In this study, to find out genes regulating ABA biosynthesis involved in abiotic stress signal transduction, the mutants with altered NCED3::LUC expression compared to wild type under drought treatment were selected for physiological characterization, functional analysis and map-based cloning of mutated genes. Among total 51 mutants investigated, one hos (for high expression of osmotically responsive gene) mutant, 1-20-1-2 and one los (for low expression of osmotically responsive gene) mutant, 1-8-2-6 were selected for further analysis. The whole size of 1-8-2-6 was smaller than wild type and sensitive to LiCl in seedling and rosette leaves. Furthermore, 1-8-2-6 was resistant to MV during germination and seedling development, but sensitive during vegetative development. 1-20-1-2 was resistant to NaCl and its root elongation was more suppressed under KCl than in wild type. RT-PCR analysis indicated that the transcript level of NCED3 gene in 1-8-2-6 was lower, but in 1-20-1-2 was higher than in wild type under dehydration, NaCl and ABA treatments as well as non-stressful condition. Under non-stressful condition, the transcript level of stress-responsive genes such as RD29A, RAB18, COR15A and RD22 were lower in 1-8-2-6 but higher in 1-20-1-2 than in wild type. Under dehydration and NaCl treatments, the expression levels of RD29A and COR15A were still a little bit lower in 1-8-2-6, whereas the expression level of RD22 was higher in 1-20-1-2. On the other hand, histochemical detection of reactive oxygen species under high light treatment in seedlings and rosette leaves of 1-8-2-6 indicated that the level of superoxide radical production was less in 1-8-2-6 than in wild type at seedling stage, whereas the levels of both superoxide radical and hydrogen peroxide production were more in mature rosette leaves. The transcript levels of superoxide dismutase (SOD) genes were investigated in 1-8-2-6 by RT-PCR analysis, that showed the expression level of Fe superoxide dismutase 1 (FSD1) was higher under MV treatment in seedlings, but it was lower under high light treatment in rosette leaves, explaining the level of the reactive oxygen species production in 1-8-2-6.In summary, 1-8-2-6 might act as a signaling component to induce the expression of NCED3 gene and also has a role in oxidative stress response by regulating the expression of FSD1 gene. 1-20-1-2 might act as a negative regulator to repress NCED3 gene expression under osmotic stresses. X선회절, 아미노산조성변화와 키틴의 함량변화를 통해 유기매트릭스의 변성을 확인하였으며 변성된 조직에서의 표면변화를 제타전위를 측정하여 조직의 반응성(reactivity)을 측정하였다. 또한 결정성장실험을 통해 유기매트릭스의 함량변화는 물론 유기매트릭스의 구조적 변화가 패각 취성의 원인인 것을 확인하였다. In nature, biogenic calcium carbonates are associated with organic substances such as proteins and polysaccharides, the principal constituents of bone and invertebrate shell organic material. Oyster shells consist of mineralized composites of calcium carbonate and macromolecules in an exquisitely controlled manner, exhibiting exceptional nanoscale regularity and strength that can overcome the intrinsic brittleness of abiotic calcium carbonate. The higher-order architecture results from the presence of the organic matter that creates defects in the perfect lattice which, in turn, enhance the fracture toughness by both absorbing stress and deviating the propagation of cracks along the cleavage planes of calcium carbonate, indicating the primary role in controlling the biominerals during nucleation and growth. It has been proposed that the interaction of proteins with specific crystal planes is governed by intrinsic stereochemical recognition of the macromolecules for specific molecular motifs exposed on certain crystal planes. On the other hand, the controlled nucleation process giving rise to an additional biological level of control in shell formation was suggested.A number of studies have been made to inspect the mechanisms of controlled crystallization of the mineralized tissue. The biological approaches have been to isolate the protein components from organisms and examine the formation of calcium salts in the presence of these components. Recently some results showed the conformation-dependent control of calcite crystal growth by a peptide of defined secondary structure, and suggested the construction a polypeptide that could bind to a specific surface of a growing calcite crystal and alter its morphology would be possible. Also, biomimetic systems under compressed monolayers of negatively charged acidic ions have indicated the importance of electrostatic binding or association, close geometric match, and stereochemical correspondence at inorganic-organic interfaces, but these have not provided adequate explanations for the controlled crystallization in biological systems. The difficulty with the above studies is that the exact nature of the molecular interaction between the crystals and the shell proteins could not be understood, and no detailed recognition and organization processes involved in the construction of these higher-order mineralized composites has yet been revealed.In this study, we specifically discuss the shell formation of the oyster, Crassostrea Gigas, which is a composite of crystallographically highly ordered calcium carbonate with organic matrices. The investigation of the structural relationship between the organic and the inorganic materials is the main subject. The approach taken was to extract quantitative information on the shell protein structure in vivo using FSD analysis of amide 1 band spectra obtained from FTIR spectroscopy.It is noted that periodic structures such as β-structure proteins aligned along specific crystallographic axes are above 54% content in each layer. It is consistent with the fact that when aspartic acid, glycin, and serine occur in proteins, more than 50% of proteins are most likely to be in the β-sheet conformation. This is known as major component of structural proteins that form geometric matching for the controlled nucleation. That is, β-structure which adopted regular repeating negative charges could bind Ca^(2+) ions. Also, it has been showed that polypeptide could have a dramatic effect on calcite crystal morphology and the effect is conformation-dependent.The first stage in biomineralization is known to involve the preorganization of supramolecular assemblies prior to mineral deposition. We noted that in forming oyster shells, water molecules and ions are likely to be bound to the active regions on the preformed intracellular spaces, resulting in appropriated protein conformational changes compared to the secreted one itself. As the shell grows in the solid state, proteins are forced into contact with calcium carbonate only. Thus, such an environment must have changed the original conformation of the protein itself and could play a key role in determining the characteristics of the secondary structure of proteins. Actually, in contacting with calcium ions from the decalcified proteins of each layer, the fact that there were more proteins in the ordered state could suggest that calcium ions cause extensive cross-linking and aggregation of the protein monomers. Though the investigation of the structural relationship between the organic and the inorganic layers is far from complete, such structural and textural changes are roughly consistent with the effect of conformation on the shell morphologyAnd in order to establish the correlation between morphological modification and polymorphic behavior, the effect of secondary structure variations on the shell formation was studied from calcium carbonate formation in the presence of proteins extracted from within shell.Under in vitro assays, the formation of protein-bound calcium ions is likely to more easily induce the nucleation of the more stable polymorph, calcite. But the mineral-specific proteins obtained from both the calcite-bearing layer and the aragonite-bearing layer induced calcite and aragonite, respectively. It indicates that a specific features of the mineral-specific proteins are essential to form each polymorph, but it is important to reminder the fact that its unique conformational information is not fully understood as yet. In this study, however, the results showed an age-related structural disaggregation process, which is thought to be readily accompanied by changes in conformation of secondary structure in denaturing of the aragonite-specific protein due to nonexistence disulfide bridge cross linking. It is notable that in the gel electrophoresis of the shell proteins, no interchain disulfide bonds that could stabilize the proteins were found to be involved in covalently linking with one another, indicating the greater likelihood of a denaturing tendency. Because of the destruction of the three dimensional protein structure as the shell grows, a particular spacial arrangement of the Ca^(2+) ion binding sites on the aragonite-specific proteins results in the change of polymorphic behavior. The phase transition from calcite to aragonite or vice versa is known as abrupt transition. That is, the transition is accompanied by a switch in production of the polyanionic proteins that control shell biomineralization. The results shown here demonstrate for the first time the existence of an entity of mineral-determining protein structure and the age-related denaturing process in controlling the polymorph.Also, the structure and texture of the shell were investigated by a wide-angle X-ray diffractometer. In particular, features of the coherence length to mineral tissues were interesting subjects to be investigated.There is a marked difference between the coherence lengths of each of them. The center layer, especially, shows a marked reduction in coherence length in all directions, consistent with the morphological features and the small volume of unit cell.Dimensional changes in the tissues would have great effect on the tissue cracking, changing the fracture stress. That is, the degree of ordering strongly depends on the coherence length. The coherence length is a function of the size of crystals along the chain axis as well as the degree of disorder in the crystals. Therefore, one would expect higher crystal size along each crystallographic direction to give higher coherence length. This feature can be correlated with the fact that the intracrystalline proteins secreted from oysters are selectively adsorbed on calcium carbonate crystals according to molecular recognition or biological activity, and this causes them to develop into biologically-formed nanocomposites. Generally, in biogenic skeletal elements, the coherence length is 1/3 to 1/4 as much as that in synthetic crystals. It means that the presence of macromolecules concentrated at coherent blocks within the biominerals is responsible for the increased toughness. In contrast, textural isotropy could not be detected in the synthetic crystals grown in the presence of shell proteins. This was then attributed to the poorly controlled environment of synthetic crystal growth, relative to the biogenic ones. The results are consistent with the fact that in nacre, biogenic aragonite contains a significant density of dislocations, contrary to abiotic one. We noted that this could reflect the biological overriding of stereochemical molecular recognition, suggesting preferential orientation of shape formation and growth of the shell.In this study, we noted that in the deformed shell, the contents of chitin and insoluble proteins were markedly decreased, which is widely believed as a framework in biomineralization. Generally, the organic matrix secreted by oysters is composed of chitin associated with proteins, which is intercalated within calcite crystals. And chitin is a polysaccharide chain composed of repeating units of N-acetyl-D-glucosamine, approximately 18 to 25 of these molecular chains assemble in narrow and long crystalline units. It can be concluded that proteins are bound to the periphery of these parallel chitin crystals, which then aggregate into fibrils, leading to the framework. But, the deficiency found in this experiment can be altered the natural shell structure to abnormal one, leading to quite different mechanical properties.In order to identify how the microscopic composition changes in organic components may affect the shell deformation such as fragility, we investigate the surface charge of biogenic calcium carbonate in the presence of intracrystalline proteins that have different compositions and structures but same origin. It is important to know if the substrate is positively or negatively charged, because with different macromolecules compositions occluded in shell, this can lead to the deformation process in a completely different stability of structure. Thus, it is of interest to investigate the interfacial reactions at the shell surface-electrolyte solution. So, we studied the zeta potentials of biogenic calcium carbonates at various solution conditions.First, it could be noted that the compositions of organic matrix occluded are entirely different between the normal and the deformed calcium carbonate, it was necessary to examine their electrochemical properties. It can be suggested that the stability of the biocomposite surface is influenced by the occluded amount and the conformation of the organic matrix. Though the protein is made up of a number of different types of amino acids, only certain amino acids can take part in the ionization reactions, which will generate a charge on the protein surface. The relevant reactions are illustrated by -COOH ⇔ -COO^(-) + H^(+) for acidic amino acid, and by -NH_(3)^(+) ⇔ -NH_(2) + H^(+) for basic amino acid. Also, calcite reactivity tends to strongly influences its surface charge and, therefore, its electrophoretic mobility and electrokinetic potential. Under the same conditions, the normal shell was negatively charged and the variation range of zeta potential is within 8mV. No isoelectric point was detected. In contrast, the range of the deformed shell is within 14mV. The main reason for such behavior of the normal shell was concluded to be the organic matrix occluded to its surface, which prevented calcite from reacting with ions and solutes from the solution, leading to more stable biocomposite comparing to deformed shell. Also the decrease of the zeta potential can be ascribed to the double layer compression due to the increase of the ionic strength as the pH was increasing. Due to the unusual composition of the macromolecules occluded on myostracum in deformed shell, there are no characteristics. Even though the compositions of macromolecules occluded in each layer are acidic, their amino acid compositions between the two type were quite different. It can be noted that the difference of nonpolar and neutral group is regarded as the dominant organic species determining of the stability of the shell. The results show clearly that the entire deformed layer with different composition of occluded organic matrix shows significant influence on the zeta potential of the suspended biogenic calcium carbonate surfaces. Thus, the results support the assumption that organic substances occluded within calcium carbonate are the dominating factor in regulating tissue properties.The results showed that it could describe the alteration of biogenic crystal texture by intracrystalline proteins having well known secondary structures, and their roles in regulating the deposition and growth of mineral crystals. Also we showed that a major biological activity rather than stereochemical recognition is present in the shell formation such as mineral transportation, deposition and transformation. That is, the effects of being modified to other conformations on the biocomposite formations are responsible for determining the unique mineralogy of biomineral. Also, we can say that in the formation of biocomposite calcium carbonate, the organic matrices, such as proteins and chitin, have the most important role. The results may potentially be applicable to synthetic strategy in the processing of highly ordered nanostructural material design
